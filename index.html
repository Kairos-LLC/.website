How it works

From "are you around?" to already knowing.

Set your pattern

Describe your week once — work hours, shift rotations, quiet time. Kairos handles the repetition, including cycles that don't fit a normal week.

Share a code

Generate a one-time code and hand it to a friend or family member. They import your schedule in seconds — no accounts, no friend requests.

Glance, don't ask

Your dashboard shows who's free right now and who's free soon, 24/7. Badges surface changes so you never plan around stale information.

Privacy

Your time is personal. Kairos treats it that way.

  • On-device first. Your schedule lives on your phone, not in a cloud profile built around you.
  • No account required. A recovery key — not an email and password — is how you back up and restore.
  • You choose who sees what. Sharing happens through one-time codes you generate and hand out yourself.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Do my friends need the app to see my schedule?

Yes — sharing works between Kairos apps. You generate a one-time code, they enter it in their app, and your availability appears on their dashboard from then on.

Does Kairos track my location or read my calendar?

No. Kairos only knows the availability pattern you describe to it. There's no location tracking, no calendar scraping, and no analytics profile built from your habits.

What happens if I lose my phone?

Restore from your recovery key — twelve ordinary words you wrote down when you set up backups. Enter them on a new device and your schedule and shares come back.

Can it handle rotating shifts, not just 9-to-5?

Yes. Kairos supports custom weekly patterns and shift cycles of any length — 4-on-4-off, alternating weekends, night rotations — so your dashboard is accurate even when your weeks aren't identical.

Can I stop sharing with someone?

Anytime. Revoke a share and that person's app stops receiving your availability. Codes are one-time use, so nothing can be re-imported without your say-so.
